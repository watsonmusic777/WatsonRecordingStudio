About Watson Recording Studio

The Story Behind the Sound

Watson Recording Studio is more than a workspace — it’s the result of a lifetime shaped by rhythm, creativity, family, and the drive to bring music to life.

My musical journey started at home. When I was around four to seven years old, my dad put a guitar in my hands. He taught me how to hold chords, how to feel rhythm, and how to listen — really listen — to the heart of a song. Throughout his life, he was my go‑to for advice. Anytime I struggled with a progression or needed guidance, I went straight to him. Those moments weren’t just lessons; they were memories, bonding, and the foundation of everything I would become.

As I grew older, music expanded beyond the living room. I joined the SGI Brass Band, one of the biggest marching groups on Oʻahu. We marched in nearly every major parade — and we didn’t just show up, we won, often beating out well‑known high schools with huge programs. That band taught me discipline, teamwork, and the power of precision.

I started on snare, moved to tenor, and eventually found my home on bass drum, where I excelled. The heartbeat of the band — that was my role. Even though I’m no longer marching, my heart is still there. Those memories shaped who I am as a musician and as a person.

Music has always been my language. It’s how I think, how I create, and how I connect with people.

My Creative Approach

Today, I bring that same energy into Watson Recording Studio — the precision of percussion, the creativity of guitar, the discipline of marching band, and the inspiration that’s been with me since childhood.
